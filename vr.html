AR

Augmented Reality


Augmented Reality (AR) is an interactive experience of combining a real-world environment with enhancements provided by computer-generated (CG) perceptual information. Unlike VR, AR is not a completely immersive, virtual experience. Virtual objects or displays lay over the real world with positional tracking, typically through a set of smart glasses, goggles, a monocle, or an HMD .This technology is especially useful for military and medical purposes as both require much focus and information in high-stress, timely scenarios. MR

Mixed Reality


In the Simulation industry, Mixed Reality refers to what is also known as Augmented Virtuality (AV). It is the addition of tangible, real-world objects to a virtual environment using external cameras. However, MR is also known as a spectrum between VR and the real world. L&D Solution Mixed Reality is anywhere between the extrema of the virtual continuum. This virtual continuum includes AR and AV. With MR technology at our fingertips, L&D Simulation can develop scenarios, missions, and environments to integrate with real cockpits, controls and devices to create effective immersion for training.
Mixed reality is the next wave in computing followed by mainframes, PCs, and smartphones and it seems going mainstream for consumers and businesses, thus liberating our screen-bound experiences by giving us instinctual interactions with data in our living spaces and with our friends.
